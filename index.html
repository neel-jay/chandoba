About Chandoba Comics

Chandoba (चांदोबा) was the Marathi edition of India's most beloved children's magazine, Chandamama. First published in April 1952, it brought the magic of storytelling to Marathi-speaking children across Maharashtra and beyond.

The magazine was founded by B. Nagi Reddy, a renowned Telugu film producer, with his friend Chakrapani as the editor. The stories were beautifully illustrated and written in the traditional "grandparent storytelling" style — drawing from Indian epics like the Ramayana and Mahabharata, mythology, fables, and the beloved tales of King Vikramaditya and Vetala (Vetal Pachisi).
